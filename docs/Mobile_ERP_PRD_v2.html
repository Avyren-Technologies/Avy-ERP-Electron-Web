1. Executive Summary

This Product Requirements Document defines the functional requirements for the Mobile ERP application — a comprehensive enterprise resource planning solution designed for small and medium-sized manufacturing enterprises (SMEs). The application runs on mobile devices and provides end-to-end operational management spanning sales, inventory, vendor relations, finance, production, human resources, machine maintenance, and security.

1.1 Purpose

This PRD serves as the single source of truth for all stakeholders including product owners, designers, developers, and quality assurance teams. It outlines what the application must do from a user and business perspective.

1.2 Scope

Sales & Invoicing — Quote-to-cash lifecycle with GST-compliant invoicing and payment tracking
Inventory Management — Stock tracking, goods receipt, material requests, and warehouse management
Security — Gate operations including employee attendance, goods verification, and visitor management
Vendor Management — Purchase orders, ASN, GRN, and vendor performance tracking
Finance — Receivables, payables, payment recording, and financial reporting
Machine Maintenance — Preventive scheduling, breakdown tracking, and spare parts management
HR Management — Employee records, attendance, leave, payroll, incentive configuration, and working hours rules
Production — OEE tracking, shift-wise production logging, scrap/NC management, and incentive computation
Masters — Centralised configuration for items, shifts, machines, operations, and parts
Visitor Management — Pre-registration, check-in/out, and QR-based self-registration

1.3 Target Users

Business Owner / Admin — Full dashboard visibility across all modules
Sales Team — Quote creation, invoice management, customer ledger
Operations / Production Manager — Production logging, OEE monitoring, shift management
HR Personnel — Employee management, attendance, payroll processing
Finance Team — Payment tracking, receivables/payables management
Security Personnel — Gate attendance, goods verification, visitor management
Maintenance Team — Preventive and breakdown maintenance operations
Vendors (External) — ASN creation and delivery coordination

2. Product Overview

The Mobile ERP is a mobile-first application providing end-to-end manufacturing operations management. It is accessible through a bottom navigation bar for frequently used modules (Home, Sales, Inventory, HR) and a side drawer menu for all modules. The application features role-based view filtering on the home dashboard.

2.1 Application Structure

Dashboard (Home) — KPI cards, role-based filtering, sales trend chart, quick-access module grid, and recent activity feed
Module Screens — Dedicated screen per module with tab-based sub-navigation
Modal Forms — Bottom-sheet modals for quick data entry actions
Detail Views — Tapping any list item opens full record with contextual actions
Search — Contextual search within each module

3. Sales & Invoicing

The Sales & Invoicing module manages the complete sales lifecycle from lead capture through quotation, invoicing, payment collection, and customer ledger management. It supports both PO-referenced and general (ad-hoc) transactions with full GST compliance based on customer location.

3.1 Sales Home

The landing tab is titled "Sales Home". It provides summary statistics — current month sales, outstanding amount, and active quote count — along with the sales pipeline and quick-action buttons.

Sales Pipeline

Active deals with customer name, product/service, pipeline stage, and deal value
Stages: Quote Sent, Negotiation, Order Confirmed
Tappable for full customer detail, contact info, and actions (Call, Create Quote)

Quick Actions

New Quote
Create Invoice
Add Customer
Inward PO (Customer PO Entry)

3.2 Invoicing

Invoice List

All invoices with status: Paid, Partially Paid, Pending, Overdue
Each shows: invoice number, customer, due date, amount, and status

Invoice Detail

Full breakdown: number, customer, dates, amount, line items, payment status
Actions: Download, Send Reminder, Payment In
Invoice Creation (PO / General Reference)

When creating an invoice, the user selects the type:

PO Reference — Select a customer PO; all line items auto-populate. User can edit quantities/prices and remove items.
General — Full manual entry: customer, dates, line items, and totals.
Payment In (Against Invoices)

Record incoming payments against invoices. The form captures:

Amount received, payment date, payment mode (Bank Transfer, Cheque, Cash, UPI), transaction reference

The system tracks partial payments and displays balance remaining. Status auto-updates to Paid, Partially Paid, or Overdue.

3.3 Quote Creation

Line Item–Based Quotes

The New Quote form supports individual line items in two modes:

From Item Master — Select from centralised list; details auto-populate (description, unit, rate, HSN, GST).
General / Manual — Enter all fields manually: description, quantity, unit, rate, and tax.

Quote includes: customer, validity date, line items, applicable GST, and total.

3.4 Customer Management

Add Customer

Form captures: company name, contact person, phone, email, and address. City and State are mandatory for GST determination:

Same state as seller → CGST + SGST
Different state → IGST

GST type is auto-applied on all invoices and quotes for that customer.

3.5 Customer Ledger

Searchable directory: total business value and outstanding per customer
Detail view with contact info, transaction history, and balance summary

3.6 Inward PO (Customer Purchase Orders)

Customer PO Entry

Record Purchase Orders received from customers:

PO number, PO date, customer name, expected delivery date
Line items: part, description, quantity, unit price, line total

These POs are available as references when creating PO-based invoices.

4. Inventory Management

Tracks all physical stock (raw materials, WIP, spare parts, finished goods) across multiple warehouses. Manages receipts, requests, and issues.

4.1 Stock View

Search by name or SKU; stats: total items, low-stock alerts, warehouse count
Low-stock alerts: Critical and Low severity
Detail: item name, SKU, qty, reorder level, warehouse, quick PO action

4.2 Goods Receipt

Goods Receipt Screen

Inward material into inventory via three reference types:

PO Reference — Select PO; pending items displayed for receipt confirmation
ASN Reference — Select ASN; shipment details and items pre-populated
Security Check Reference — Link to gate verification record from Security module

User confirms quantities, notes discrepancies/damages; inventory updates on confirmation.

4.3 Material Requests

Internal requests: department, item, quantity, date, reason
Approval workflow: Pending → Approved/Rejected

4.4 Material Issues

Tracks materials issued to departments with issue number, department, items, and status

5. Security

Gate-level operations: employee attendance, goods verification, and visitor management. Used by security personnel at the facility entrance.

5.1 Attendance Screen

Employee Attendance at Gate

Two capture methods:

Manual Entry — Enter employee code; system records punch (in/out) with timestamp and shows name confirmation.
Auto Attendance via Face Scanning — Camera identifies employee; auto-records punch with name/photo confirmation.

Real-time checked-in count for current shift. Quick search by name/code. Data flows to HR module.

5.2 Goods Verification Screen

Gate-Level Goods Verification

Two methods:

ASN-Based — Enter/scan ASN number; auto-displays vendor, PO, items, vehicle, driver. Security confirms.
Manual Invoice — Enter invoice number and vendor name. Goods marked "verified at gate."

Record becomes available in Inventory module for formal goods receipt.

5.3 Visitor Verification Screen

Visitor Gate Management

Displays today's expected visitors from pre-registration. Security can:

View details: name, company, purpose, host employee
Verify and approve — triggers notification to host
Handle walk-in visitors

5.4 QR Code–Based Visitor Self-Registration

Visitor Self-Registration via QR

Security displays/prints a QR code. Visitor scans it and fills a web form:

Name, company, phone, purpose of visit, person to meet

Submitted info goes to host for approval. On approval, security is notified to allow access.

6. Vendor Management

Handles procurement: vendor records, PO creation, ASN, and GRN.

6.1 Vendor Directory

Vendors with star ratings and on-time delivery percentages
Detail: contacts, total orders, monthly spend, quick PO action

6.2 Purchase Orders

PO Creation with Item Master Integration

Line items pull from the Item Master. Only Purchase/Both items appear. On selection: description, UOM, HSN, and GST auto-populate. PO form: vendor, delivery date, line items, quantities, rates, total. Status tracking: Confirmed, In Transit, Partially Received, Completed.

6.3 Advance Shipping Notice (ASN)

ASN Creation

References a PO; line items pre-populate. User confirms quantities being shipped. Includes: vehicle number, driver name, expected arrival. ASNs feed into Security (goods verification) and Inventory (goods receipt).

6.4 Goods Receipt Note (GRN)

References ASN. Captures: received qty, condition (Good/Damaged/Partial), remarks
Detail: GRN number, vendor, items, completion status

6.5 ASN Verification

Pending verifications with vendor, vehicle, status; linked to Security workflow

7. Finance

Tracks receivables and payables, records payments, and generates financial reports.

7.1 Dashboard

Summary: total payables, total receivables, payment progress, paid vs. pending

7.2 Payables

Dedicated list: vendor, invoice, amount, due date, ageing (Current, Due Soon, Overdue)
Payment Made (Vendor Payments)

Record outgoing payments: vendor, invoice, amount, mode, date, reference. System updates payable balance.

7.3 Receivables

Dedicated list: customer, invoice, amount, due date, ageing. Actions: Send Reminder, Record Payment
Payment In

Record payments received against invoices: select invoice, enter amount, mode, date, reference. System updates balance and status.

7.4 Separate Receivables & Payables

Distinct tabs/sections for independent management. Overdue items prominently highlighted in both.

7.5 Financial Reports

Profit & Loss Report
Balance Sheet
Cash Flow Statement

8. Machine Maintenance

Preventive maintenance scheduling, breakdown tracking, and spare parts management for maximum uptime.

8.1 Machine Master

Machine Registration & PM Scheduling

Register machines and configure preventive maintenance:

Machine name, code, location, manufacturer, model
Frequency: daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, annually
Tasks per interval with estimated duration and required spare parts

8.2 Maintenance Schedule

Preventive Maintenance Execution

Due tasks in two views:

List View — Sortable/filterable: machine, task, date, status (Pending, In Progress, Completed, Overdue)
Calendar View — Monthly view with task density indicators; tap date for details

Start maintenance, record observations, mark completion, log replacement parts.

8.3 Breakdown Management

Report: machine, issue type, description, priority (Critical/High/Medium/Low)
Active breakdowns with downtime tracking and assigned team
Detail: machine, issue, reported time, downtime, team, resolution

8.4 Spare Parts Inventory

Spare parts with SKU, applicable machines, stock qty, and low-stock alerts

9. HR Management

Employee records, attendance, leave, payroll, incentive configuration, and working hours rules.

9.1 Employee Directory

Searchable list: name, department, designation, employee ID
Stats: total staff, present, on leave
Detail: full info with Call/Email actions. Add new employee form.

9.2 Attendance

Daily dashboard: present, absent, on-leave counts
Data sourced from Security module's gate attendance
Working Hours Configuration

Define minimum hours: Full Day (e.g., 8 hrs) and Half Day (e.g., 4 hrs). System auto-categorises attendance for payroll. Below half-day threshold = absent.

9.3 Leave Management

Pending requests with approve/reject workflow and employee notifications
Detail: employee, type, dates, days, reason

9.4 Payroll

Monthly summary: employees, gross salary, deductions, net payable
Payroll Master Configuration

Per-employee salary structure:

Basic Salary, Allowances (HRA, Conveyance, Special, etc.), Deductions (PF, ESI, Tax, TDS, Loan EMI), Bank Details

Basis for automated monthly payroll calculation.

9.5 Incentive Configuration

Production-Based Incentive Rules
Part Selection (Multiple) — One or more finished goods
Operation Selection (Multiple) — One or more operations
Machine Selection (Multiple) — One or more machines
Target Production Quantity — Base target before incentives apply
Range-Based Slabs — E.g., 101–120 = ₹5/pc, 121–150 = ₹8/pc, 150+ = ₹12/pc

Auto-calculated from Production module data.

10. Production

Shop-floor management: OEE monitoring, shift-wise production entry, incentive computation, and scrap/NC tracking.

10.1 Machine OEE Dashboard

OEE Monitoring

All machines with OEE metrics:

Availability — Running time vs. planned time
Performance — Actual vs. ideal production rate
Quality — Good units vs. total (scrap/NC deducted)

Colour indicators: Green (≥85%), Amber (60–84%), Red (<60%).

10.2 Production Input

Production Data Entry

Each entry captures:

Shift (from Shift Master), Employee, Part (multiple), Operation (multiple), Machine, Quantity

Feeds: shift/machine reports, OEE, and incentives. Supports batch entry.

10.3 Performance Summaries

Daily — Production vs. target, top performers, incentive earnings
Weekly — Aggregate units, target, total incentives
Monthly — Aggregate units, target, total incentives

10.4 Scrap & NC Entry

Scrap and Non-Conformance Recording

Per day or shift:

Date, shift, machine, part, operation, quantity, rejection reason, reworkable/scrap

Feeds into OEE Quality component.

11. Masters Module

Centralised configuration hub for all reference data. Ensures consistency across modules.

11.1 Item Master

All items: code, name, description, UOM, HSN/SAC, GST rates, category
Classification: Purchase, Sales, or Both — controls item visibility across modules

11.2 Shift Master

Shifts: name, start/end times, planned downtime, net production time
Assignable to production lines or entire facility

11.3 Machine Master

All machines: code, name, location, manufacturer, model, specs
Linked to PM schedules; referenced in Production and OEE

11.4 Operation Master

Manufacturing operations (turning, milling, grinding, assembly, etc.)
Linked to parts for production tracking and incentive calculation

11.5 Part Master

Per-part configuration of which operation = "Finished Good"
Drives Finished Goods Available KPI on Home Screen

12. Home Screen

Central dashboard with at-a-glance KPIs, quick module access, and activity feed.

12.1 KPI Cards

Today's Sales — Daily sales value with % change
Finished Goods Available — Parts completed final operation minus dispatched/invoiced
Pending Payments — Outstanding payables with invoice count
Pending POs — Open POs with ASN verification count
Finished Goods Calculation: When a production slip records the configured final operation for a part as completed, that quantity is added to Finished Goods. Displayed value = completed final operations − dispatched/invoiced quantities.

12.2 Role-Based Views

Role selector: All, Owner, Sales, Operations, HR, Security. Adjusts visible KPIs, modules, and feed.

12.3 Quick Access

Eight module icons: Sales, Inventory, Vendors, Finance, Maintenance, HR, Production, Visitors.

12.4 Sales Trend

7-day bar chart of daily sales values.

12.5 Recent Activities

Chronological feed of events across modules. Each activity tappable for detail view.
